Chapter Summaries

  • Act 1: Scene 1
  • Hamlet opens by introducing Bernardo and Marcellus, two nigh watchmen, on a particularly cold night
  • The two men are standing outside the Danish palace in which they are meant to protect
  • The audience learns that on the previous two watches the men have witnessed a peculiar sight, the ghost of the former Danish king, King Hamlet
  • The two watchman welcome a man onstage by the name of Horatio
  • Horatio has just arrived to Denmark and he is prepared to help the men verify the ghost
  • Horatio is skeptical at first on whether the ghost will appear, however not long after his arrival Hamlet's ghost appears
  • The two watchmen pressure Horatio to speak to the ghost, however Horatio holds of from speaking and the ghost eventually disappears
  • A story is told on stage about a man by the name of Fortinbras. Fortinbras' father was rumored to have been killed in combat by Hamlet
  • A rumor has been going around that Fortinbras is planning to invade Denmark
  • Following this story the ghost of Hamlet returns once more and Horatio finally speaks to him
  • Following this last visit Horatio and the other watchmen agree that they should inform Prince Hamlet about the appearance of his fathers ghost
  • Act 1: Scene 2
  • ï‚§This scene begins at the court of Claudius and Gertrude, the King and Queen of Denmark.
  • The next scene opens at the court of Claudius and Gertrude, they are the current rulers of Denmark
  • Claudius discusses the possible invasion of Fortinbras' men
  • Claudius, who has spoken to Fortinbras' uncle, the King of Norway, says that Fortinbras' has promised to avert his attack on Denmark
  • Prince Hamlet chimes in on the topic finally
  • Hamlet seems very sullen and gloomy in his words and Claudius questions his nature
  • Claudius then adds that Hamlet should move on past his fathers death, and that prolonged grief does no good
  • He also adds that he would like Hamlet to forgo returning to his schooling in Wittenberg, and instead remain in Denmark with the King and Queen
  • Hamlet decides that he will stay in Denmark
  • Hamlet proceeds to go into a soliloquy, he explains his deep grief, and states that a lot of reasoning behind it has to do with his mother's quick remarriage with Claudius
  • When Hamlet is done speaking, Horatio, Bernardo, and Marcellus enter the stage
  • Horatio at firsts begin to discuss the state of Denmark, but then he finally tells Hamlet of seeing his father's ghost
  • Act 1: Scene 3
  • The play shifts to Laertes and Ophelia
  • Laertes is making plans to leave, however before setting off he discusses his sisters relationship with Hamlet
  • Laertes hopes his sister will end the relationship, because nothing good can come out of it for her as Hamlet is the prince of Denmark
  • Before Laertes can leave his father, Polonius arrives and adds to the conversation
  • He states that all the declarations of love that Hamlet has mad are only in attempt to take Ophelia's chastity from her
  • Polonius forces Ophelia to promise that her days of romance with Hamlet are over
  • Act 1: Scene 4
  • The scene shifts back to the night watch where Hamlet along with Horatio and Marcellus wait for the ghost
  • Eventually the Ghost arrives and Hamlet immediately tries to speak to it
  • The ghost however tries to leave, yet Hamlet pursues it
  • Horatio and Marcellus are worried that Hamlet will come to harm by following the ghost, so they in turn follow Hamlet
  • Act 1: Scene 5
  • Hamlet is finally able to speak to the ghost of his father
  • The ghost claims that he was not killed by a snake as had been the story, but in fact murdered by Claudius who now holds the throne
  • The ghost claims that Claudius snuck up on him while he was asleep in his garden and poisoned him
  • The ghost tells Hamlet he must seek vengeance, but only towards Claudius and not his mother
  • Hamlet vows to seek revenge, and then has Marcellus and Horatio swear that they will keep what they just heard secret, no matter how odd Hamlet's actions may seem in seeking revenge
  • Act 2: Scene 1
  • The act opens with Polonius sending a man, Reynaldo, to spy on his son on his journey
  • Reynaldo's job is to learn about any powerful Danes that are currently in Paris, and all the while make sure he does not let anyone know his connection to Laertes
  • Reynaldo is also to discuss with people Laertes in a non conspicuous way, and mention that Laertes is fun loving young man who enjoys himself an occasional fight, chances to gamble, whores, and alcohol
  • Polonius believes he has prepared Reynaldo and sends him on his way
  • Meanwhile Ophelia enters and explains to her father that Hamlet has been acting strangely
  • Act 2: Scene 2
  • The King has also noticed Hamlet's strange antics and decides to investigate
  • Claudius brings forward two friends of Hamlet Guildenstern and Rosencrantz to see if they can discover anything about Hamlet
  • In the meantime Polonius rushes forward to the King to explain that he believes Hamlet's madness to be the result of a failed attempt to woo Ophelia
  • The scene shifts to the two friends of Hamlet, they walk in on Hamlet and the friends have a long friendly conversation
  • Hamlet is suspicious by their sudden arrival and questions them believing they are sent by the King and Queen
  • Hamlet explains to them why he acts so
  • He then tells of a plan to determine Claudius' guilt
  • Hamlet plans for a play to be presented to Claudius in which certain lines will be read that should incite an expression of guilt if he in fact killed King Hamlet
  • Act 3: Scene 1
  • The two friends return to Claudius but lie to him and say they are still unsure of why Hamlet acts maligned
  • They add to Claudius that Hamlet seemed excited about the play that will be occurring that night
  • Hamlet enters the stage after the exchange between Claudius and his friends
  • Hamlet proceeds to give his most famous lines, the "To be or not to be" rant
  • While during his speech Hamlet notices Ophelia is present
  • He proceeds to rant at her and tells her to leave him alone as he wants nothing to do with her, he even suggests she go become a nun
  • Hamlet leaves following his rant and Ophelia is in shock
  • Claudius and Polonius appear and it is assumed that they were hiding and were witness to Hamlet's speech
  • Claudius decides that to control Hamlet's odd behavior he will send him to England
  • Act 3: Scene 2
  • Hamlet is back on stage this time speaking to the actors in the upcoming play and giving them tips on how to act naturally when reading the lines directed towards Claudius
  • Hamlet then seeks out Horatio and asks that he help him determine the Kings reactions to the lines of the play
  • Horatio sits away from Hamlet and in view of the King
  • Meanwhile Hamlet takes his seat near Claudius, Polonius, Ophelia, and Gertrude
  • Before the play begins he makes sure to take time to harass Ophelia
  • The play ensues and proceeds to play out in the described way of King Hamlet's murder
  • The plot confuses some but Hamlet instructs them to listen to the upcoming prologue
  • The actors that play the king (king hamlet) and queen (Gertrude) enter the stage
  • They proceed with a dialogue in which they discuss their long and wonderful marriage
  • The king then proceeds to tell the Queen that if he shall die that she must remarry
  • The Queen is disturbed by this saying she shall only love the King
  • Hamlet turns to his mother, the real Queen, and asks her opinion of the play
  • She responds with the famous line "The lady doth protest too much, methinks"
  • The play then resumes with a character coming and murdering the king with poison, the character then tries to win the love of the Queen
  • Claudius has seen enough and orders the play to end
  • Hamlet and Horatio are both pleased as they now know that the ghost was telling the truth
  • The stage empties save Hamlet who delivers a soliloquy
  • Hamlet explains that he shall treat his mother cruelly for her marrying a murderer, however he shall not harm her
  • Act 3: Scene 3
  • Polonius makes a brief appearance on stage informing the audience that he plans on listening in on a conversation that Hamlet is about to have with his mother
  • Polonius exits the stage and the King enters, alone
  • Claudius expresses shame with himself and his actions, he kneels upon the ground in prayer and asks to receive freedom from his guilt
  • Deep down he is aware that he will not receive any forgiveness as he still holds claim to the prizes awarded to him through his act
  • Claudius remains in prayer and Hamlet enters the stage pausing behind Claudius, unbeknownst to him
  • Hamlet ponders taking the chance to exact his revenge, however he resolves to instead wait until a moment when he finds Claudius committing sin, not a moment of prayer
  • Hamlet continues on and enters his mothers chamber
  • Meanwhile Polonius is making sure to hide himself so he can listen in (Polonius hides behind a curtain in Gertrude's chamber)
  • Once inside Hamlet confronts his mother about her sinful acts of marrying her husbands brother
  • He is ashamed with her and he lets her know
  • Gertrude fears that Hamlet will resort to harming her and cries out for help
  • In response Polonius cries out as well from behind the curtain
  • Hamlet believes that it is in fact Claudius hiding and stabs the curtain only to reveal a dying Polonius
  • Hamlet realizes his error but is not too upset as Polonius was intruding
  • In an effort to convince his mother of her wrong ways Hamlet presents two portraits to her, one of the deceased King Hamlet, and one of Claudius
  • He explains that the portrait of Hamlet represents nobility and honor, while the portrait of Claudius represents sin and evil
  • Gertrude finally sees the error of her ways
  • Following this King Hamlet's ghost appears once more, but he is only visible to his son
  • The ghost tells Hamlet to return his focus to Claudius
  • Hamlet obliges
  • Meanwhile Gertrude witnesses the exchange yet cannot see the ghost, she in turn is convinced her son is mad
  • Act 4: Scene 1
  • Hamlet leaves the room and takes Polonius' body
  • Claudius witnesses this and immediately asks Gertrude what has happened
  • She explains the accidental murder
  • Claudius seeks out Hamlet's two friends again and asks that they track him down
  • Act 4: Scene 2
  • When they arrive to question Hamlet he casually shrugs off any of their questions and tells them that the King is leading them on
  • Act 4: Scene 3
  • begins with Claudius and Hamlet speaking to one another
  • Claudius inquires to the whereabouts of Polonius' body and Hamlet nonchalantly replies to where he has put it
  • Claudius is furious and confused with Hamlet and demands he leave for England immediately
  • Hamlet mocks him on his way out of the room
  • Claudius is left alone and thinks aloud about having Hamlet killed in England
  • Act 4: Scene 4
  • ï‚§For the first time in a while the play shifts to Fortinbras
  • His large army has assembled at the borders of Denmark
  • While Hamlet is being led away from Denmark by Rosencrantz and Guildenstern he manages to speak with the captain of the army
  • Hamlet inquires the armies intentions and learns that they are seeking a small piece of land that they claim they own in Poland
  • Hamlet seems that it is ironic that the army would risk death for a small and pointless piece of land yet when he has seemingly something important to fight for he cannot seem to act
  • Hamlet vows to finally plan to finish the deed of killing Claudius
  • Act 4: Scene 5
  • The scene shifts back to the kingdom in Denmark
  • Gertrude is being informed that Ophelia has gone mad after her father has gone missing
  • Claudius enters the stage and comments that perhaps it wasn't wise to cover up his death
  • Meanwhile Laertes, upset with his fathers death, approaches the kingdom with plans to claim the throne
  • Claudius stops him and explains that he too is upset by the death and that Laertes has no right to the throne
  • Act 4: Scene 6
  • Still back at the court of the King and Queen, Horatio takes the stage
  • He announces that he has received a letter from Hamlet
  • The letter explains that Hamlet has been captured by pirates who seek favors in exchange for his life
  • Act 4: Scene 7
  • The stage shifts back to Laertes and Claudius who are speaking with one another
  • It seems as if Claudius has calmed down Laertes and the two have resolved their differences
  • A messenger appears on stage and hands a letter to Claudius, the letter is from Hamlet and explains that he will be coming to the court soon
  • The two men agree that when Hamlet arrives Laertes will duel him and Laertes being the better swordsman should be able to kill him
  • The scene is then interrupted by Gertrude who comes bearing bad news, Ophelia has drowned
  • Laertes is shattered and Claudius attempts to console him
  • Act 5: Scene 1
  • The final act of the play starts and two gravediggers are on stage preparing for Ophelia's burial
  • Horatio and Hamlet join them on stage
  • Hamlet remarks at the indifference the gravediggers seem to be displaying in light of the job they partake in
  • One of the gravediggers removes a skull from the ground and explains to Hamlet that it is the skull of a former jester, Yorick
  • Hamlet takes the skull in his hand and begins to give a speech about Yorick as he once knew him
  • Hamlet is soon interrupted by Claudius, Laertes, and Gertrude who have arrived to lay Ophelia to rest (they are unaware that Hamlet is present however)
  • Laertes is extremely distraught during the burial, even jumping in the grave and asking to be buried with her
  • Hamlet presents himself to the group and Laertes is shocked
  • The two seem to wrestle for a bit, as Laertes blames Hamlet
  • Hamlet explains that he had a deep love for Ophelia, and that perhaps he loved her more than Laertes did
  • Hamlet then proceeds to leave and Claudius and Laertes counsel and plan to kill Hamlet
  • Act 5: Scene 2
  • Hamlet receives a letter from Claudius proposing a contest of swordsmanship between Laertes and he.
  • Hamlet is given a letter that turns out to be from Claudius
  • The letter proposes that Laertes and Hamlet duel
  • Horatio is skeptical as he is aware of Laertes skills with a sword, but Hamlet is confidant
  • The dual is prepared and a crowd gathers to watch
  • The duel begins with a point going in Hamlet's favor
  • After the first round Hamlet is offered a poisoned drink, however he refuses
  • They dual again and Hamlet wins the next round
  • Following that round Gertrude decides to give a toast to Hamlet yet drinks the poison drink by accident
  • Claudius is the only one aware of this
  • Hamlet and Laertes return to dueling, yet the round ends in a draw
  • Following the round Gertrude collapses and dies leaving Hamlet in shock and off-guard
  • Laertes takes advantage of this and stabs Hamlet with a poisoned sword
  • Hamlet manages to wrestle the sword away and stab Laertes as well
  • Hamlet then proceeds to stab Claudius and then forces him to drink the poison
  • Claudius collapses and dies
  • Laertes also begins to collapse and in his dying breaths asks for forgiveness
  • Hamlet and Horatio are left but Hamlet is dying
  • Hamlet asks that Horatio clear his name and also ensure that Fortinbras becomes King of Denmark
  • Fortinbras arrives on stage
  • He has returned from Poland and realizes what has occurred at the Kingdom
  • Fortinbras decides to give Hamlet a proper burial and then takes over the throne
  • The play ends with Hamlet being taken away for his funeral
