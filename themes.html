Themes

  • Hamlet's quest for certainty
  • Unlike most plays and tragedies in this time period Hamlet takes a very long route to seek out his revenge
  • Hamlet first would like to be certain about all situations such as whether Claudius is in fact the killer, and also would like to be certain in his plans of revenge
  • Hamlet's quest for certainty before revenge seems to prevent him from promptly and precisely exacting his revenge, and ultimately leads to the death of the whole royal family
  • Action and its consequences
  • Another theme throughout is action and how if it is taken unwisely can result in consequences
  • Hamlet unlike many others in the play seems to contemplate his actions, however this prevents him from acting and therefore Hamlet chooses to act in a blind and uncontrolled manor
  • Hamlet believes that those who do as they please and do not stop to think have the worst results
  • A prime example is Claudius who intends to kill Hamlet, Claudius plans to use a poisoned rapier to kill Hamlet, however the rapier is turned on himself killing Claudius
  • The influence of the royal family on the nation as a whole
  • This theme is displayed during the whole play, originally when King Hamlet is in power the nation is very well off and productive as the royal family is strong and powerful
  • Following his death the nation and the royal family is in turmoil as it is uncertain of the new leader
  • All throughout the play the nations well being reflects the stability of leadership
  • The concept of death
  • Following his father's death, Hamlet is obsessed with understanding death as well as gaining revenge for his father
  • Hamlet thinks to himself about what becomes of the dead, for example ghosts and what are they, as well as contemplates what becomes of human remains
  • Hamlet also contemplates suicide and the idea of his own death is very pressing on Hamlet, he believes death will end his suffering but suicide would further suffering in the afterlife
  • The idea of death resonates throughout the novel
